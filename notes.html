Nova Notes

The terms, the trends and the small habits that separate a set that lasts two weeks from one that lasts four. No fluff, just the stuff worth knowing before you book.

01Know your terms

The words on the menu, explained

Everything on the price list uses these terms. Here's what they actually mean.

Gel X A full-cover soft gel extension: a pre-shaped tip is bonded over the natural nail, then cured, shaped and designed. Featherlight, no drilling, and it soaks off gently when it's time for a change.
Magnets How nail length is measured on the menu, from XShort (0 to 1 magnets) up to XLong (8 to 10 magnets). A quick, consistent way to compare length across every style.
Art tiers How nail art is priced, from Tier 1 (simple accents) through to Tier 4 (complex 3D, charms, hand-painted detail). The tier is confirmed from an inspo photo before every appointment.
Lash mapping Splitting the eye into zones and assigning a length and curl to each one, like a blueprint, so the finished set suits the eye shape it's built for rather than a generic chart.
Classic, hybrid, volume Classic is one extension per natural lash (Nova bare, Nova doll). Hybrid mixes single lashes with light fans for texture (Nova silk, Nova dewy). Volume uses handmade fans for full density (Nova luxe, Nova Elite).
Refill vs new set A refill tops up a lash set that still has enough extensions left, priced lower than a full set. Past the three week mark, a fresh full set gives the best result.
2 in 1 Lashes and nails, done in one appointment, by the same technician. One sitting, both looks planned together.
Duo appointment Two friends, two chairs' worth of attention, one time slot. Book your own services and go through the appointment side by side.
Removal Lash extensions are dissolved with a professional remover; Gel X soaks off in acetone. Neither is pried, picked or drilled, which is what actually protects the natural lash or nail underneath.
02Choosing your set

Matching a set to your actual life

The best set is the one that fits how you use your hands and how bold you actually want to feel, not just what's trending this week.

Nails

Length + shape

  • Typing, gym, hands-on work? XShort to Medium keeps things practical while still looking finished
  • Event, formal, or a photo-heavy weekend coming up? Long to XLong is where the drama lives
  • Shape is a free choice: duck, square, stiletto, coffin or almond, none of them cost extra
  • Not sure? Send an inspo pic when booking and the length gets talked through first
Lashes

Style + vibe

  • Want it to read as "your lashes, just better"? Nova bare or Nova doll keep things classic
  • After texture and flutter that photographs well? Nova silk or Nova dewy build in wisp
  • Inspo folder says full glam? Nova luxe or Nova Elite bring maximum density
  • Every style is mapped to your eye shape on the day, so the same style can look different (and right) on different faces
03Trend glossary

What's actually trending, decoded

The looks living on the feed right now, and what to say when you're booking one.

Chrome

A metallic, mirror-like powder finish over Gel X. Reads as futuristic or icy depending on the base colour underneath.

Cat eye

A magnetic gel polish pulled into a shimmering line with a magnet before curing, giving a shifting, almost 3D sheen.

French tip

A classic white or coloured tip over a sheer base. Still one of the most requested looks on the menu, in any colour combination.

3D + charms

Hand-built florals, pearls and small charms set into the gel for texture that lifts off the nail rather than sitting flat.

Wispy

The Nova silk lash effect: longer spikes layered through a soft base for texture without heaviness. The most requested lash look on the menu.

Mega volume

Dense, ultra-fine handmade fans (Nova Elite) for maximum lash density. Built for events, shoots and main-character energy.

Milky / dewy

A soft, semi-sheer milky pink or nude base for nails, or the glossy piecey "wet look" spike for lashes (Nova dewy).

04Aftercare 101

The five-second version

The full routine lives on the experience page. Here's what to remember before you even leave the chair.

Lashes

Keep it dry, keep it clean

No steam, saunas or swimming for the first 24 hours, then a daily oil-free cleanse and a gentle spoolie brush keep a set full and fluffy.

Nails

Oil is everything

Cuticle oil daily is the single biggest thing you can do for wear time. Gloves for dishes, and nails are jewels, not tools.

Both

Never pick, never peel

A lifted tip or a loosening lash gets fixed with a message, not fingers. Picking is how natural lashes and nails actually get damaged.
